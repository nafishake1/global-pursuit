The Game

What is it?

A daily deduction game. A criminal is on the run. You track them across 4 cities by interviewing witnesses and reading clues — then tapping the right city on a spinning globe. Like Wordle's daily ritual, built around geographic reasoning rather than recall.

Session length

2–5 minutes. One case per day. Share your score without spoilers.

Target platform

Mobile-first web app, packaged as iOS + Android via Capacitor. Touch is the primary input.

The Vibe

Tone direction

Campy and theatrical — 90s Carmen Sandiego energy. Bold, witty, playful. The criminal is a larger-than-life globe-trotter who's always one step ahead — until suddenly, they're not. The emotional register is like a punchline landing: theatrical reveals, fanfare, a villain caught mid-escape.

NOT tense or grim. NOT cheerful or casual. Fun, precise, and satisfying.

Design Constraint — Criminal Visibility

The criminal is never shown. No avatar, no face, no portrait. They exist only as an absence — felt through witness accounts, Director briefings, and the trail they leave behind. Do not design criminal character art for MVP.

Wrong guess flow

You tap a wrong city → flight animation takes you there → a witness delivers an archetype-voiced wrong-city response (e.g. Café Owner: "I know every face that sits at my tables. That face has never sat at my tables.") → the city greys out on the globe → you pick again. No lives lost. Wrong guesses reduce your efficiency score. Wrong-city responses are drawn from a per-archetype pool — see characters GDD for full variants.

Witness reveal

3 witnesses per city, tap one at a time. Witness 1 = broad signal. Witness 2 = narrows it. Witness 3 = precise. You can guess after any witness.

Scoring

Path Efficiency %. Fewer witnesses used + fewer wrong guesses = higher score. You always catch the criminal — the question is how cleanly.

The Globe

The game board — try it

The globe IS the selection UI. No city cards, no lists. Players spin to find highlighted pins anywhere in the world and tap to guess.

3 cities · find them all
Drag to spin · Tap a city to guess

The mechanic: City pins are scattered globally — the criminal's route is globe-spanning (Paris → Tokyo → Oslo → Rio). Players spin to discover pins. Only highlighted cities are tappable. Wrong cities grey out after the narrative response. The hunt for pins is part of the experience, not friction.

Discoverability

Gold arrows at the globe edge point toward off-screen pins. Counter shows how many exist. Players always know more pins are out there.

Mobile-first

Touch drag to spin. Tap to select. Hit targets are larger than visual pin size. City name labels appear above each pin.

Screens

Screen states

💬 CIA Director = Text thread

The case brief and capture confirmation arrive as a text message thread from the Director. Intimate, theatrical, spy-fantasy. The dramatic tone lands perfectly as a direct message.

🎭 Witnesses = Scene cards

Each witness reveal is a scene card — a location environment (café, taxi, airport gate) with the witness character present as a silhouette. Clue delivered as a caption overlay. Three scenes per city — each one narrows the trail.

Visual System

Two distinct layers

Signal icons — clue card

~25–30 SVGs rendered from signals[] on each witness clue. Appear alongside clue text as reading aids. The player still reasons from signal to city — icons don't give away the answer.

City visuals — globe pins

Optional per-city SVGs (flag, landmark, artifact, cultural dress, currency). Authored once per city. Appear on globe pins — never on clue cards. City pins show names only, preserving deduction.

Design Pillars

The five pillars

🔍

1. Deduction Over Trivia

Every answer must be reachable by reasoning. No question has "just know it" as the only path. Clues describe signals; the player connects them to geography.

⚖️

2. Tension Without Cruelty

Wrong moves cost efficiency, not lives. You always catch the criminal. No fail states.

⚡

3. Snappy Above All

Sub-2s load. Instant transitions. Zero perceptible lag. Performance is a design value, not an afterthought.

📤

4. Clean Social Proof

Share card creates FOMO without spoilers. Results communicate skill without revealing the route.

🎭

5. Juice Without Lag

Every correct action is rewarded immediately — visually, sonically, haptically. Animations fire on input, never after. State never waits for feedback. The register is theatrical and campy — the dramatic reveal, fanfare, a villain caught mid-escape. Punchline landing, not alarm sounding.

Your Role

Design deliverables

🌍
Globe Component Design
Visual language for the interactive globe: sphere + grid style, pin states (default, hover, selected, greyed-out), flight arc style, edge indicator arrows. This is the hero element of the game.
🎨
Signal Icon Set
~25–30 SVG icons for the signal taxonomy. Legible at 20px. Consistent style — slightly illustrated, not purely flat. Taxonomy must be locked before case authoring begins.
🏙️
City Visual Assets
Optional per-city SVGs: flags, landmark sketches, artifacts, cultural dress, currency. Prioritise the 30-city MVP set. Landmark illustrations must be evocative but not name the place.
📱
UI Component System
Figma components for: witness cards (3 states: locked, revealed, dim), clue card with signal icon strip, globe overlay, case brief, wrong-guess screen, capture screen, result + share card.
🎬
Motion Principles
Animation language: flight arc style, pin reveal, wrong-guess fly-to sequence, capture payoff, share card reveal. Timing values map to Framer Motion configs.
🔤
Design Tokens
Colour, type, spacing, motion values as Figma Variables. Exported as tokens.json → CSS custom properties in the app.
🖼️
Art Direction Bible
Mood board, colour direction, illustration style guide. Criminal character(s) — illustrated villain silhouette or per-case avatar? CIA Director — illustrated or text-only for MVP?

Signal icon style — Filled, outline, or duotone? Should feel consistent with campy/theatrical tone — slightly illustrated rather than purely flat.
